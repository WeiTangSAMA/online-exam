# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The system serves three groups: administrators who maintain the exam platform, teachers who manage question banks, papers, scoring and analytics, and students who enter exams, answer under time pressure, and review scores. Users are usually in a school, training, or coursework context where clarity, trust, and speed matter more than decoration.

## Product Purpose

Online Examination System provides a complete exam workflow: question management, paper composition, online exam taking, automatic grading, score management, and data analysis. Success means each role can quickly find the right task, understand system state, and complete exam-related work without confusion.

## Brand Personality

Clear, steady, efficient. The interface should feel like a polished teaching operations tool: calm enough for exam work, refined enough for a finished project, and direct enough for repeated daily use.

## Anti-references

Avoid toy-like dashboards, generic gray admin templates, oversized decorative heroes, flashy gradients, glassmorphism, and visual effects that compete with exam tasks. Do not make student exam flows feel like marketing pages or games.

## Design Principles

- Lead with task clarity: the current role, next action, and system state should be obvious.
- Make hierarchy decisive: important counts, actions, and navigation should carry more visual weight than supporting detail.
- Keep trust in the surface: restrained color, consistent controls, and readable contrast matter more than decoration.
- Use real product artifacts: cards, tables, exam metadata, and analytics should provide the visual interest.
- Preserve flow under pressure: students should not fight the UI during timed exams.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast for text and controls. Keep keyboard and focus states visible through Element Plus defaults and custom states. Avoid motion that delays task completion, and respect reduced-motion preferences for non-essential transitions.
